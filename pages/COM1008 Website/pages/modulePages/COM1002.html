COM1002 | Foundations of Computer Science

This module is worth 20 credits in the first level of the degree. It introduces the basic mathematical studies needed for later years in the degree. It is used to increase logical thinking within the students and to demonstrate why they are important to the computing world.
The assessment for this module takes forms of MOLE quizzes, problem sheets and an examination in the second semester.
Examples of things you will cover are:

  • Propositional Logic
  • Set Theory
  • Predicate Logic
  • Functions
  • Mathematical Proofs
  • Backus-Naur Form

Sessions are given in forms of lectures and lab sessions enhancing the content learnt.
