COM1006 | Devices and Networks

This module is worth 20 credits in the first level of the degree. It introduces computer architecture to students which shows the low level operations of a CPU and memory. Students also learn how to program in assembly language, the lowest language you can get to without actually programming in binary. Students will also learn about how devices are connected in networks and introduces protocols and error detection.
The assessment for this module takes forms of MOLE quizzes, and an end of year examination covering everything from the two semesters.
Examples of things you will cover are:

  • Digital Logic
  • Computer Arithmetic
  • Boolean Algebra and sequential circuits
  • Instruction Set architecture
  • Assembly Language programming
  • The structure of the CPU

Sessions are given in forms of lectures and lab sessions enhancing the content learnt.
