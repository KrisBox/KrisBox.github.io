COM1005 | Machines and Intelligence

This module is worth 20 credits in the first level of the degree. It introduces the information on Artificial Intelligence and key concepts and problems in the field. From the history to briefly looking how vision, natural language and others concepts work. You also will have an experience using NAO robots learning how to program and change their properties.
The assessment for this module takes forms of a MOLE quiz, small assignments and an end of year examination.
Examples of things you will cover are:

  • Look into the historical and cultural context of modern day research.
  • To gain experience in scientific programming
  • Programming the NAO robots effectively
  • To program search, inference and planning algorithms

Sessions are given in forms of lectures and lab sessions enhancing the content learnt.
