About Me

My name is Kristofer Box and I reside from a small town in Lancashire called Burnley. I am 18 and currently studying the first year of Computer Science at the University of Sheffield. I previously attended a college in Burnley named 'Burnley College' where I achieved my A Levels in the Summer of 2015. Now currently living in the village of Endcliffe accommodation with 9 other flat mates, I am quickly reaching the end of my first semester. Whilst studying at the university, I wish to become an efficient programmer and web based specialist.

Whilst not at the university I live in a medium sized town house with my mother, step father and sister. Named Susan, Graeme and Amie respectively, we all reside from Burnley and have 2 cats called Max and Claude. The rest of my family are spread across the country in Manchester, Newcastle, Cambridge. Close relatives of mine also live in New York in the United States of America.
After my unfortunate parent split, my father moved to Cambridge and married my step mother Allison, who has two children called Sam and Matt. He moved closer to my Grandma in Cambridge and has stayed there ever since.

My home town, Burnley, is situated in the east of Lancashire in the Pennine hill range. A 15 minute drive from West Yorkshire and half an hour drive from Manchester, it has close connections to major hubs. As a popular industrial and football town, proudly supported by Prince Charles himself, Burnley has a rich heritage and history. The popular Pendle hill is located closely to Burnley making it a good location for walking hobbyists.

Fun Facts!

The odd spelling of my name, Kristofer, is derived from the famous actor and singer Kris Kristofferson. This odd name spelling is a theme in the family as my sisters name is spelt Amie instead of the conventional Amy.

Burnley FC is most known for its involvement in the creation of the Football League with 11 other teams in 1888. Even to this day, they are still progressing well in the Football League.

In high school, I was surprisingly given the role of Head Boy for year 11 despite the popularity and expectations of other students leaving mass arguments amongst other students, a very fun year indeed!
